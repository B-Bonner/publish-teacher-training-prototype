Which letter will successful applicants receive?

Successful candidates will receive the following email from UCAS:

{{ data['training-provider-name'] }} will assume you are taking up your place, and will be sending you joining instructions before you start your training programme.

In UCAS this is known as the “No response” type of letter, with the code: GT12

Receive email alerts for new applications?

Yes. Emails are sent to janedoe@myorg.co.uk as soon as new applications are received.

How should applicants be sorted in your outstanding decisions list (ODL)?

Alphabetically

You will find your outstanding decisions list in the applicant data section of UCAS web-link.

There are outstanding decisions lists for applicants:

  • awaiting a decision from you (On-demand ODL)
  • who will be rejected by default over the next 7 days (Weekly ODL)
  • who have accepted conditional offers but are awaiting confirmation decisions (Confirmation ODL)

What type of Star J (*J) return is needed?

Flat file

Star J (or *J) is a report that must be returned to the Higher Education Statistics Agency (HESA) at the end of each recruitment cycle. It contains personal information of each accepted applicant.

The return is available either as a flat file or as a UCAS link.

Sending statistical information about each applicant to your training provider (Star X)

Send information. Statistical data will be sent to: janedoe@myorg.co.uk

You can choose to send extra statistical information about each applicant to your training provider.
